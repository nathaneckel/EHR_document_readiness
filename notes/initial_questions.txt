# Initial Business Questions

## Core Question
Can document readiness failures be categorized early enough to reduce operational friction for healthcare staff and families?

## Questions for Initial Exploration

1. Which failure type appears most often?
2. Which trigger events most often expose document problems?
3. Which failure types are most associated with high staff time impact?
4. Which scenarios are most likely to create downstream delay?
5. Which document types appear most vulnerable to confusion?
6. How often does authority mismatch create delay even when documents exist?
7. Which cases appear most preventable upstream?

## Initial Hypotheses

- Missing documents will be common, but authority mismatch may create the most confusion.
- High-urgency events will expose inaccessible or outdated documents more often.
- A relatively small number of failure patterns may account for a large amount of staff friction.
- Many document issues are not random; they become visible only at predictable trigger points.

## Why This Is a Business Analysis Project

This project focuses on:
- failure mode classification
- process friction
- workflow timing
- preventable operational delay
- structured requirements for future interventions
